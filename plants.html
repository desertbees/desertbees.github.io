Plants

Here are some native plants that we have encountered while sampling.

Encelia farinosa

Encelia farinosa, commonly known as brittlebush can be found in a variety of desert habitats, in sand or gravel. Read more at Calflora.

Krameria bicolor

This is Krameria bicolor. Commonly known as white rhatany, it generally grows near the creosote bush, Larrea tridentata. Read more at Calflora.

Larrea tridentata

Creosote bush, or Larrea tridentata is a common evergreen shrub. One of the oldest living organisms on the planet, King Clone, belongs to this species of plant. Read more at Calflora.

Marina parryi

Parry's false prairie-clover is the overly-long common name for Marina parryi. It is a small legume plant with striking deep purple flowers. Read more at Calflora.

Olneya tesota

Desert ironwood, or Olneya tesota is a favorite of mid-season bees. These desert trees tend to stand out; they can grow up to ten meters high, have grey bark and light purple-pink flowers. Read more at Calflora.
