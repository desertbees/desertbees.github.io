Maps

Here are some maps showing historical distributions of a few bee species found in the desert. To better see the maps, please open the images in a new tab.

Anthophora edwardsii

Anthophora edwardsii has a widespread distribution, ranging from the central valley of California to northern New Mexico.

Habropoda miserabilis

Habropoda miserabilis is found sporadically, mostly in coastal areas.

Habropoda pallida

Habropoda pallida is a true desert bee, found mostly in the Mojave, Colorado, and Sonoran deserts.

Habropoda tristissima

Habropoda tristissima can be found throughout California.
